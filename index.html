Get Conference Updates Learn More

Why This Conference?

Article V of the U.S. Constitution provides a mechanism for citizens and states to propose amendments through a constitutional convention. As interest in this tool grows, fundamental questions about how such a convention should be designed remain largely unexplored.

This conference brings together legal scholars, constitutional experts, and practitioners to examine core procedural issues: How should delegates be selected? How many states does it take to trigger a convention? How do we prevent a "runaway" convention? What transparency and ethics safeguards should apply?
